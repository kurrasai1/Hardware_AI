## 📖 Overview

This challenge involved studying a recent state-of-the-art review paper on neuromorphic computing and writing a reflective report answering five in-depth questions about its future, challenges, and breakthroughs.

## 📚 Article Reviewed

**Kudithipudi, D., Schuman, C., Vineyard, C.M., et al. (2025).**  
*Neuromorphic computing at scale*. Nature, 637, 801–812.  
🔗 https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-08253-8

## 🧠 Learning Goals

- Understand the current landscape and obstacles of neuromorphic computing at scale.
- Reflect on critical features like sparsity, scalability, and benchmarks.
- Propose future directions for research and development in neuromorphic hardware and software.

## 📝 Questions Answered in the Report

1. **What is the hardest feature to scale in neuromorphic systems?**  
   → Argued that neuronal scalability is the most challenging due to hardware constraints and power limits.

2. **What could be the "AlexNet moment" for neuromorphic computing?**  
   → Described potential breakthroughs that could spark widespread adoption.

3. **How to bridge the gap between neuromorphic hardware and software?**  
   → Proposed a standardized middleware framework ("Neuromorph-OS") for interoperability.

4. **What unique benchmarks should we use to evaluate neuromorphic systems?**  
   → Introduced metrics like Spike Energy per Inference and Latency to Spike.

5. **How can emerging memory tech (memristors, PCM) enhance neuromorphic computing?**  
   → Discussed co-located memory-compute benefits and research pathways.

## ✅ Key Takeaways

- Neuromorphic computing presents promising alternatives to conventional AI for edge and low-power computing.
- True breakthroughs require cross-layer collaboration in hardware, algorithms, and system-level software.
- With scalable architectures and better development tools, neuromorphic systems can drive the next generation of adaptive computing.
